Electricity

Electricity is a form of energy that powers almost everything we use in daily life—from lights and computers to trains and factories. It’s created by converting other types of energy, such as the movement of water, wind, or steam, into electrical energy that can travel through wires to homes and businesses. In modern society, electricity is essential for communication, healthcare, education, and nearly every industry. Without it, many of the technologies and conveniences we rely on every day wouldn’t exist.

Whats on the website

This website was created to help people learn about the different ways electricity is made around the world. Each page focuses on one type of energy generation—like coal, hydro, solar, or wind—and explains how it works, what its advantages and disadvantages are, and how it affects the environment. There’s also a page where two energy types are compared side by side to see which might be more efficient, affordable, or sustainable.
